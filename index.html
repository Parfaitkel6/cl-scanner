The problem

Scam sites look real. That's the whole trick.

Convincing logos and checkout pages don't tell you who actually runs a site, how long it's existed, or whether it's been flagged before. By the time something feels wrong, you've already entered your card.

01

New sites, no history

A domain registered last week can look identical to one that's been trusted for a decade.

02

Hidden ownership

Privacy-shielded WHOIS records make it impossible to see who's actually behind a storefront.

03

No single source of truth

Checking a certificate here, a blacklist there — most people don't have time to piece it together.
